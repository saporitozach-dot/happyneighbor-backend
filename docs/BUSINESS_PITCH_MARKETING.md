# Happy Neighbor — Marketing Section (Business Pitch)

*12pt font | 1 page max*

---

**M1. Ideal Customer**

*Macro:* First-time and repeat homebuyers in the U.S., ages 25–45, with household income of $75K–$200K, who are actively searching for a home within 6–18 months. Focus on suburban and urban markets where lifestyle and community matter.

*Micro:* People who care about finding a community, not just a house. They value neighbor connections, walkability, safety, schools, and local events. They are willing to trade some convenience or price for a better “street fit,” and are open to tools that help them make more intentional choices about where they live.

---

**M2. Market Size**

There are approximately 5–6 million existing-home sales per year in the U.S., plus millions of renters considering homeownership. Narrowing to lifestyle-driven buyers who actively research neighborhoods yields a target of roughly 2–3 million households annually. This is enough to build a sustainable consumer and partnership business.

---

**M3. Reach Strategy**

Happy Neighbor will reach customers through partnerships with realtors and real estate sites (e.g., listing syndication, referrals); SEO and content around “neighborhood matching” and “find your community”; social media and LinkedIn where lifestyle and community content resonates; and local marketing in priority metros. Free tools (survey, matches) and easy sign-up will support awareness, consideration, and conversion.

---

**M4. Message**

**“Find a street that feels like home.”** Happy Neighbor helps you find communities, not just houses—places where you fit based on noise, walkability, safety, schools, and community vibe. We use verified resident input to match you with neighborhoods that match your lifestyle, so you can make a smarter move and build real connections.

---

**M5. Marketing Challenges & Mitigation**

*Trust and data quality:* Residents may be reluctant to share neighborhood reviews. We address this with LinkedIn verification, clear privacy controls, and incentives for participation.

*Competition from Zillow/Realtor.com:* Large incumbents focus on property data, not community fit. Happy Neighbor positions itself as a complementary lifestyle layer, not a replacement, and we will explore integration partnerships.

*Reaching buyers early:* Many people start with price and location. We use content and partnerships to reach users when they’re ready to think about lifestyle, and we offer a free, low-friction survey to capture interest early.
